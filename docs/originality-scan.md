# Originality scan — Café Zack

**Date**: 2026-05-23
**Question**: Has any 3D personal portfolio been built that combines Algiers/MENA cultural specificity, a suited-agent character with a face-reveal moment, a drivable vintage taxi, pipeline-as-physical-world visualization, a fictional café for the services menu, and a data engineering brand?
**Method**: 10 targeted web searches across Awwwards, FWA, three.js forum, R3F gallery, GitHub, Codrops, Muzli. See sources at end.

---

## Findings by axis

| Axis | Existing landscape | Café Zack position |
|---|---|---|
| **Algiers / Maghreb / MENA 3D personal portfolio** | Zero hits across Awwwards, FWA, three.js forum, R3F gallery. Bruno Simon stands alone globally as the high-watermark for 3D portfolios; no MENA equivalents exist. | **First** |
| **Real named-city landmarks as navigation** | Generic 3D cities and procedural city generators exist (`mauriciopoppe/Three.js-City`, `jstrait/city-tour`, `3DStreet`). None use real named landmarks of a *specific* city as section anchors in a personal portfolio. | **First** |
| **Data engineer 3D portfolio** | Zero. Every DE portfolio surfaced was a dashboard, GitHub README, or Streamlit/Tableau page. Awwwards has never featured a DE personal portfolio. | **First** |
| **Pipeline-as-physical-world visualization** | Kafka/Airflow visualization exists (Confluent Stream Lineage, SoftwareMill viz, Aiven tool) but only as engineering tools — never as the *world structure* of a portfolio. | **First** |
| **Suited-agent character with face reveal** | None found in portfolio context. Some games and cinematics use the device; no portfolio has. | **First** |
| **Fictional café / restaurant for services menu** | Restaurant brands use menus on their own sites; pop-culture fictional restaurants (Royal Sushi 8-bit kaiju, Central Perk fan sites) exist. No PORTFOLIO uses a fictional café as the services container. | **First** |
| **Drivable vintage car portfolio** | One canonical (Bruno Simon's buggy). Our Peugeot 504 is a culturally specific reskin, different city + agent + theme. | Second on the car axis alone |
| **Walking-character 3D portfolio** | Many (Henry's Room, multiple R3F examples on three.js forum). Common pattern — not differentiating on its own. | Crowded |

## Closest precedents (worth knowing, not blocking)

- **HekTek City v4** (Nov 2025, three.js forum) — open-world cyberpunk portfolio in R3F. Closest in *spirit* (persistent 3D world as portfolio), but cyberpunk-themed, fictional city, no DE angle, no café metaphor, no cultural anchoring. Not a conflict.
- **Killian Herzer** (Awwwards Honorable Mention, April 2026) — interactive developer portfolio with a "cinematic archive" and a "3D simulation". Different scope (abstract simulation, not real city), no cultural specificity, not data-themed.
- **Bruno Simon's 2025 Portfolio** (Awwwards SOTM Jan 2026) — the driving-car archetype; cited in every 2026 portfolio trend article. Sets the bar we're targeting on physics + audio + interactivity but is the *thing we're differentiating from* on car-only.
- **Arnaud Rocca** (Codrops case study, March 2026) — GSAP motion system + fluid WebGL. Motion-artistry direction, not exploration.
- **Samsy Ninja (SMSY)** — Paris-based creative technologist mixing 3D + computational design. Abstract creative coding, no city / character / DE crossover.
- **Carles Faus Arquitectura** — "Spatial Silence" architecture portfolio, Mediterranean white-space approach. Different medium (architecture studio) but interesting reference for restraint.

## Why the combination is genuinely novel

Each individual axis above is either uncontested (DE 3D, pipeline-as-world, fictional café, named-city, MENA cultural) or has at most one canonical example (drivable car = Bruno Simon, walking character = several). **No portfolio combines more than two of our axes.**

The result is unique by composition, not by inventing a new primitive — which is the realistic kind of "first of its kind" for a one-person build.

## Recommendation

Lean **hardest** into the two completely uncontested axes:

1. **Pipeline as the physical fabric of the world** — see plan: "The world IS the pipeline" section. This is the single most distinctive design move available to a DE specifically. No portfolio has done this.
2. **Cultural specificity of Algiers** — real ochre walls, real call to prayer at sunset, Arabic neon, the yellow Peugeot 504, named landmarks (La Grande Poste, Casbah, Notre-Dame d'Afrique, Maqam Echahid). No portfolio has ever centered an Algerian/Maghrebi identity. This is *free* differentiation that money can't buy elsewhere.

Soft-pedal axes where we're not first:
- The drivable car is great but credit Bruno Simon openly; lean on the Peugeot 504 / taxi-yellow cultural specificity rather than the *driving* itself as the novelty.
- The walking-character mechanic is common; the *suited-agent-with-reveal* narrative is the differentiator, not the walking.
